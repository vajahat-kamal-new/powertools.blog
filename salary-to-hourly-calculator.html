How to Use This Calculator

Converting a salary to an hourly rate takes three inputs: your annual salary, your weekly hours, and how many weeks you actually work per year.

  • Annual Salary — Enter your gross (before-tax) yearly pay.
  • Hours Per Week — Most full-time jobs are 40 hours. Adjust if yours differs.
  • Weeks Per Year — Standard is 52. If you take unpaid time off, enter how many weeks to subtract.
Hourly Rate = Annual Salary ÷ (Hours/Week × Working Weeks)

For example: a $75,000 salary with 40 hours/week and 2 weeks unpaid leave = $37.50/hour.

Why Convert Salary to Hourly?

Understanding your true hourly rate is one of the most useful things you can know about your compensation — here's why:

Compare Job Offers Fairly

A $90,000 salaried job with 50-hour weeks pays less per hour than an $80,000 job with 40-hour weeks. The hourly rate gives you an apples-to-apples comparison.

Evaluate Freelance Opportunities

If a client offers you a project rate, compare it to your current hourly rate. Many salaried workers undercharge when they go freelance because they never knew their real hourly value.

Negotiate Smarter

Knowing your hourly rate helps when negotiating raises, overtime, or contracts. It anchors your value to something concrete and defensible.

Budget More Accurately

Thinking in hourly terms helps you evaluate purchases differently — that $200 item costs you 5 hours of work at $40/hour. It changes how you spend.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert salary to hourly rate?
Divide your annual salary by the total number of hours you work in a year. Total hours = weekly hours × working weeks. For a standard full-time employee: $60,000 ÷ (40 × 52) = $28.85/hour.
Is the hourly rate shown before or after tax?
All figures shown are gross (before tax). Your actual take-home pay will be lower depending on your tax bracket, state, and deductions. For after-tax estimates, use the IRS withholding estimator alongside this tool.
What if I get paid time off (PTO)?
If your employer pays you for vacation days, leave the "weeks off" field at 0. You are paid for 52 weeks even if you take time off. Only enter weeks in the vacation field if those are unpaid weeks where you receive no salary.
How much is $50,000 a year per hour?
$50,000 ÷ 2,080 hours (40 hrs × 52 weeks) = $24.04 per hour. If you take 2 weeks unpaid leave, it becomes $50,000 ÷ 2,000 hours = $25.00 per hour.
How much is $100,000 a year per hour?
$100,000 ÷ 2,080 hours = $48.08 per hour for a standard 40-hour, 52-week work year. This is a common benchmark for professionals evaluating six-figure job offers.
Can I use this calculator for part-time work?
Yes. Simply enter your actual weekly hours (e.g. 20 for part-time). The calculator will accurately reflect your hourly equivalent regardless of hours worked per week.
